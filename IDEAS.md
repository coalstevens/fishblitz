## Story & World

### Premise
- [ ] The player arrives in a region organized around the four seasons
- [ ] Each season introduces new characters and local problems
- [ ] At the end of each season, a disaster strikes, forcing the player to respond
- [ ] The disasters gradually reveal that the region's balance has been damaged for a long time

### Opening
- [ ] Start: Winter→Spring transition
- [ ] Player awakens on side of road (fell off wagon)
- [ ] Road becomes unusable (washed away by raging rivers/floods) — explains no rescue
- [ ] Slow, cold, tired struggle through forest to find abandoned hut
- [ ] Sleep first night there
- [ ] Wake up cold/hungry — slowed movement, no energy
- [ ] Rusty axe next to rotting firewood outside hut
- [ ] Flint nearby as fire starter
- [ ] Wet wood won't light — must search for dry wood
- [ ] Nearby area is highly explorable (think Dark Souls / Stardew)
- [ ] Fire lit inside shed overnight → don't wake up cold (energy bar?)

### Themes
- [ ] No "bad people" — no bandits, no vagabonds — people are not out to hurt you
- [ ] Only "bad luck" — the environment is working against you
- [ ] Main seasons: chronic problems (hot summers, cold winters)
- [ ] Shoulder seasons: catastrophic events, somewhat random, with subtle foreshadowing
- [ ] Player should realize damage will happen but couldn't prepare enough
- [ ] "Restoring nature" is not about undoing all human activity, but about learning how to live within limits
- [ ] Keep environmentalism in the background — frame it as a local community drama and a cycle of adaptation and repair
- [ ] The land is strained by years of expansion, extraction, and short-term survival — not instant or cartoonish evil
- [ ] The player's role is not to forcibly restore nature, but to help people and ecosystems adapt, repair, and rebalance together
- [ ] No omnipotent reset, no single heroic fix — progress comes from choices, tradeoffs, and cooperation

### Presences
- [ ] Ancient, limited, observant — emotionally resonant rather than all-powerful
- [ ] Appear in dreams or sacred places to warn about imbalance
- [ ] Point toward forgotten practices, represent the land's memory
- [ ] Reward care, restraint, and restoration
- [ ] Guides and witnesses, not commanders
- [ ] Deliberately ambiguous — maybe they are real, maybe not
- [ ] Presences never speak directly — NPCs interpret them differently, old stories contradict
- [ ] One culture sees a blessing, another sees a warning — same sign, different meaning
- [ ] NPCs tell stories of presences they've encountered or heard about
- [ ] Farmers say the river "turns away" when people waste water
- [ ] Hunters say the forest "holds its breath" before fire
- [ ] Elders say winter "remembers" who respects it
- [ ] Children leave offerings at stones because "something listens"
- **The River Presence**: ripples where there should be none, fish gathering in a dry bend, voices heard near wells, locals refusing to cross certain fords
- **The Forest Presence**: burned trees sprouting strangely, deer avoiding a specific grove, ash falling in patterns, people claiming the woods "noticed"
- **The Winter Presence**: frost appearing on sheltered ground, tracks that vanish overnight, a distant figure seen in blizzards, old songs about the cold "taking stock"
- [ ] Never confirm the presences in a way that removes doubt — belief and reality stay intertwined
- [ ] Repair a shrine → rain returns, but so does the seasonal storm cycle (cause or coincidence?)
- [ ] Leave an offering → a path opens, but it could just be that a landslide finally cleared
- [ ] Someone sees a figure in the floodwater — no one else does
- [ ] Presences are real enough to matter, but never so direct they become the solution — they operate as omens, memory, and pressure from the land itself
- [ ] A wisp of wind or a shift in the landscape uncovers a box — reveals a goal tied to that presence

### Progression Arc
- **Early game:** Survival — just trying to survive the seasons
- **Mid game:** Build a civilization — people arrive and help build a community
- **End game:** ? (avoid Stardew rip-off)

### NPC Roles
- [ ] People arrive slowly based on time, events, or player development milestones
- [ ] People contribute roles: Blacksmith, Lumberjack, Mason, Tailor, Seed-master, Miller
- [ ] Baker shows up, takes over mill, makes bread — free bread in morning
- [ ] Tradesman (infrequent backpacking hiker)
- [ ] Herbalist — sells medicinal herbs, healing potions, remedies
- [ ] People ask for items/help — assisting speeds their progress, but they succeed eventually either way
- [ ] People defend their own stuff — you can help protect them

### Key Characters
- [ ] **Farmer** — wants to save failing crops, but their land is slowly changing because of upstream damage
- [ ] **Ranger** — tracking the spread of blight or fire, but discovers illegal resource use
- [ ] **Engineer** — trying to fix irrigation or flood control, but their solutions have unintended effects
- **Patrick (the Brother)** — morally grey logger who's been missing for years after exploring north with Maron.
- **Maron (The Guide)** — Ferrywoman of The Way North; deeply connected to the river
- **June (Crippled Sister)** — Sister of patrick. A woodworker now. Searched for her brother with Maron for years. Has a wooden prosthetic leg after an accident on the river.

### The Way North
The Way North follows the river north through the region, the only route deeper into the wilderness. The player gathers resources, fights enemies, and progresses through different biomes that grow increasingly arctic the further they go.

#### Map Progression
- 5 maps before fork, difficulty scales with depth
- The Way North — river gorge leading north, basic environment (stone, wood)
- Caves — clay, stone, can be completely flooded
- Forest — lots of wood
- Random biomes, but only from previously discovered pool
- Heavy-object chest blocking progress in each area — must meet chest requirements to proceed
- Maron ferries players along The Way North
- Skinnable maps for more environments
- Spawning characteristics change per biome

#### Combat — Bow & Arrow
- Bow with knockback, hold to charge power (like birding net)
- When the wheelbarrow gets hit by an enemy projectile, it drops an item; arrows pass right over it when empty
- More accurate when standing still; early bows require standing still
- Tool that blasts nearby projectiles away
- Magnet that deflects steel bullets (defense-only, saves arrows)
- Arrows that reflect other projectiles
- Bow types vs ammo types (maybe just bow types)
- Flying enemies (Stardew-style bats) — knockback to keep them away, costly reload

#### Stealth
- Enemies have sightlines — player can sneak through areas
- Crouch / take cover (3rd box reward)
- Arrow cost encourages sneakiness

### Trail of the Brother
As the player explores north along The Way North, he unravels the story of June, her brother, and Maron.

**Patrick (the Brother)**
  - A logger who stripped the land unsustainably, blind to the damage he was causing
  - Spent nights camped on the riverbank while using it to transport logged trees
  - Over time, dreams of treasure to the north began to surface — felt like his own ambition, an omen 
  - Hired Maron for the job, paid her to guide him north
  - His ambition did not falter, but he felt that Maron was losing motivation. That was what he thought at least.
  - Was killed by Maron before he ever reached the gold — never understood what changed in her

**June**
  - Loved her brother but never knew the full scope of what he was doing.
  - Was distraught when he went missing. She hired Maron and spent years searching for him.
  - Trusted Maron implicitly — never suspected her of anything
  - Notices Maron's silences but reads them as shared grief, not guilt
  - A cave rockfall crushed her leg, it was amputated and she replaced it with a wooden prosthetic of her own making.
  - The cave rockfall was accepted as simple tragedy — she never blamed Maron
  - Finds peace on the river now, doesn't seek answers anymore

**Maron (The Guide)**
  - Had always been drawn to the river, it felt alive to her
  - Patrick had hired her on and off to scout out logging opportunities.
  - Her and Patrick grew up together in the same town, but they weren't close.
  - Took the job from Patrick, seemed to be consistent work for while. 
  - Started feeling atypical anger, twisted thoughts about how Patrick's damage to the forests was affecting the river.
  - Killed him in a moment that felt both her own and not her own
  - Nobody ever found out that she kill him, he was assumed missing.
  - After his death the anger vanished. She felt only guilt.
  - Spent years searching for with his sister June afterward, hoping to undo what she'd done or find some sign he'd survived
  - Eventually, an unfortunate rockfall crippled June. Maron doesn't trust her own actions, she assumes she caused the rockfall somehow.
  - June still comes for boat rides once and a while. June thinks their friends, Maron doesn't think she can be her friend.

**The River Presence**
  - The river prescense was angered by the destruction of the forest, hurting the river.
  - It's anger was immense, but it does not want Patrick dead, it cares for life.
  - It wanted to lure him away from the forests he was destroying, toward a gold cache that would satiate his greed.
  - Whispered secrets of gold into his dreams on the nights he slept by the water.
  - Used Maron as a gentle hand to drive him further north because she was already open to the river's voice
  - Underestimated its own anger — centuries of damage bled through, corrupting Maron's intent
  - Watched Patrick die without meaning for it to happen, an ancient thing learning it can make mistakes
  - Feels guilty it cannot rectify what it did to Maron.

**The Player**
  - Observes Maron's hesitations and deflections without context at first
  - Witnesses the river's strange behavior around Maron — parting water, following her commands
  - Hears June's stories of old expeditions without realizing the weight behind them
  - Discovers the truth through scattered campsites and journal entries, but each piece is filtered through its author's bias

## Season System

### Drought
- [ ] Drought conditions during summer months
- [ ] Water sources dry up — rivers, ponds, wells become scarce
- [ ] Foraging and farming yields reduced during drought
- [ ] Increased wildfire risk during drought periods
- [ ] Conflicts over remaining water resources

### Main Seasons
Spring, Summer, Fall, Winter

### Transition Seasons (10 days each)

**Winter → Spring**
- [ ] Raging rivers
- [ ] Widening rivers
- [ ] Slush
- Prevention: stormwater drainage, sandbags (storm dikes), planting trees to support banks (prevents erosion/expansion)

**Spring → Summer**
- [ ] Thunderstorms
- [ ] High winds — headwinds/tailwinds affect player speed
- [ ] Isolated trees more likely to fall
- [ ] Falling trees
- Prevention: hedge rows as windbreaks

**Summer → Fall**
- [ ] Forest fires — trees burn, worsening wet seasons
- [ ] Grass fires — dry grass burns easily
- Prevention: fire-resistant plants (Douglas Fir, Cedar, grasses), douse flammables in water, rainwater collection, redirect river to form manmade lake

**Fall → Winter**
- [ ] Heavy rain → standing water → moving through deep water costs energy
- [ ] Mudslides
- [ ] Freeze → ice expansion damage, slippery, kills plants
- Prevention: ?

## Wood Types & Fire Ecology
- [ ] Western Larch — most fire-resistant tree, lays lots of seeds after fire
- [ ] Garry Oak / Aspen — resprout from intact root systems after fire
- [ ] Black Spruce — requires heat to reproduce, very flammable, doesn't recover quickly
- [ ] Fireweed
